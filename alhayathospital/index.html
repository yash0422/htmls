Investment Thesis — Why This Asset, Why Now
  • 1
    DHA license is the primary moat. A new entrant requires 18–24 months and AED 5M+ to obtain a DHA multi-specialty hospital license. This asset transfers it on day one.
  • 2
    25+ insurance empanelments took years to build. Getting empanelled with DAMAN, AXA, Allianz, and 22 other networks requires track record, infrastructure, and clinical governance that cannot be replicated quickly. Medically, this is the hardest barrier to entry in Dubai healthcare.
  • 3
    280 surgeries/month is a proven, recurring revenue engine. At an average blended ticket of AED 11,200, surgical revenue has the potential to generate significant IP income — to be verified against DHA returns and insurance submissions. The OT infrastructure (2 major + 1 minor) is fully equipped and operational.
  • 4
    Oud Metha Metro adjacency is irreplaceable. The facility is directly adjacent to the Oud Metha Metro Station — the single most accessible healthcare location in central Dubai, serving Bur Dubai, Karama, Deira, and DIFC catchments simultaneously.
  • 5
    AED 85M is replacement cost — the business comes free. Rebuilding this hospital from scratch costs AED 46.5M+ in physical assets alone, before DHA licensing, staff recruitment, and 25,000 patient relationship development. The acquirer effectively buys the entire OpCo at no additional cost.
  • 6
    Rent efficiency is extraordinary. AED 1,788,000 annual rent on 20,000 sq ft in Oud Metha represents 9.9% of audited FY2024 revenue — the most capital-efficient hospital occupancy ratio in the Dubai market.

Why Dubai Private Healthcare Is a Compelling Investment — Five Macro Drivers
  • 1
    Population growth is structural, not cyclical. Dubai's population is projected to grow from 3.3M to 5.8M by 2040 under the Dubai 2040 Urban Master Plan — a 76% increase that directly expands the insured patient pool for every private healthcare operator in the emirate. ↗ u.ae
  • 2
    Private healthcare spending is growing at 9.5% annually. EY MENA and Praxis Global Alliance independently confirm UAE private sector healthcare CAGR at 9.5% — nearly double the overall hospital market rate. This reflects the ongoing shift from public to private care and the growing preference for premium, insured services. ↗ ey.com
  • 3
    Medical tourism is an untapped revenue stream. Dubai Health Authority has set a target of 500,000 medical tourists annually. Aesthetic surgery, dental, and wellness procedures are the primary demand drivers — all of which are core to this asset's service offering. This asset currently generates zero medical tourism revenue, representing pure upside. ↗ dha.gov.ae
  • 4
    Mandatory health insurance is a permanent structural floor. Dubai law mandates health insurance for all residents. With 90%+ of Dubai's population being expatriates — and insurance coverage expanding under DHA mandates — the insured patient base grows automatically with population. This asset holds 25+ active insurance empanelments, making it immediately accessible to the insured majority. ↗ dha.gov.ae
  • 5
    Chronic disease and lifestyle prevalence is driving sustained demand. EY MENA reports 68% of UAE adults are overweight or obese, with nearly 1 in 5 adults living with diabetes. Demand for surgical intervention, specialist OPD, and preventive care is structurally elevated — and growing. ↗ ey.com

Oud Metha Patient Catchment — Location Intelligence
Why This Location Is Irreplaceable
  • 📍
    Metro Station adjacency — unique in Dubai mid-market healthcare. The facility is directly adjacent to the Oud Metha Metro Station on the Red Line, providing walk-in access from Bur Dubai, Al Karama, Deira, Al Nahda, and onward connection to DIFC and Downtown. No other mid-market hospital in Dubai holds this transit advantage. ↗ rta.ae
  • 👥
    South Asian expatriate majority — the largest insured patient segment in Dubai. The Oud Metha, Bur Dubai, and Karama catchment area houses the highest concentration of Indian, Pakistani, and Sri Lankan expatriates in Dubai — a population of approximately 1.2M people who are predominantly employed, insured, and actively seeking mid-market private healthcare. This is precisely the patient demographic this asset is built to serve. ↗ Dubai Statistics Centre
  • 🏙️
    Dubai Healthcare City Phase 1 — the most medically dense corridor in the UAE. Oud Metha sits within and adjacent to Dubai Healthcare City, home to over 120 healthcare facilities, 4,000+ licensed practitioners, and the highest concentration of health insurance claims processing in Dubai. Being licensed and operating in this corridor confers a regulatory and reputational premium that no peripheral location can replicate. ↗ dhcc.ae
  • 💼
    DIFC and Downtown professional workforce — the aesthetic and wellness segment. Within 2km, DIFC houses 4,500+ companies and 35,000+ professionals with premium health insurance and high discretionary spend on aesthetic procedures, dental, and wellness. This segment drives the hair transplant unit, cosmetic surgery, and premium dental revenue — services with the highest ticket sizes and lowest insurance dependency. ↗ difc.ae
  • 🏥
    25,000+ patient files — years of relationship capital no new entrant can replicate. The existing patient database represents the single most defensible competitive asset in this transaction. Building a patient base of this scale in Oud Metha from zero would require 5–7 years of operation and AED 10M+ in marketing and patient acquisition investment — at minimum.
