Round 1 — Pre-NDA · Send Before Signing Anything · 14-Day Deadline
Strategy: These 6 questions are conversational and non-threatening. Each one is a dealbreaker screen in disguise. If answers are not received within 14 days — treat as a red flag. If any answer is RED — stop and reassess before proceeding to NDA.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 1
I hope this message finds you well. As we progress with the sale process, there are a few points we'd like your input on — nothing formal at this stage, just helping us build a clearer picture. If you have any management figures for 2025, even a rough summary, that would help us understand the current trading position. Alongside this, a copy of the DHA hospital license would allow us to confirm the details and expiry date. We'd also appreciate a brief sense of your motivation for the sale — buyers always ask, and the more we understand, the better we can represent your interests. On that note, buyers we are speaking with are looking for a genuine partnership, not just a transaction. Having you available in an advisory capacity for 12 to 18 months post-completion would be a significant positive. On the operational side, a monthly breakdown of surgical cases for the last 12 months — from OT logs or insurance submissions — would allow us to present the volumes confidently. Finally, could you confirm whether the insurance empanelments are held in the company name and transferable on a change of ownership? Please share only what you are comfortable with at this stage. We look forward to your response.
1
FY2025 Management Accounts
"To help us move forward, could you share the management accounts for 2025 — even if unaudited? A monthly or quarterly P&L breakdown would be very helpful to understand the current trading position."
🔴 Red if: Revenue is declining or surgical income has dropped significantly · 🟢 Green if: Growth continues from FY2024 trajectoryImportant
2
DHA License Certificate
"Could you confirm the DHA hospital license number and share a copy? We want to confirm the license is in the company name, check the expiry date, and ensure there are no conditions or notices attached."
🔴 Red if: License expired, suspended, in personal name, or has unresolved DHA notices · 🟢 Green if: Current, clean, 12+ months validityDealbreaker
3
Reason for Sale
"Just to understand the background — what is the primary reason for considering a sale at this point? And would you be open to staying on in a transition capacity for 12 to 24 months post-completion to help the new owner settle in?"
🔴 Red if: Vague answer, regulatory pressure hinted, or flat refusal to transition · 🟢 Green if: Genuine retirement/liquidity, open to handover periodDealbreaker
4
Surgical Volume — Verified Data
"You've mentioned 280 surgeries per month — that's a strong number. Could you share the monthly surgical breakdown for the last 12 months? This could be from DHA surgical returns, OT booking logs, or insurance claim submissions."
🔴 Red if: Actual verified average is below 180/month · 🟢 Green if: 240+ average with upward trend — entire IP revenue model depends on thisDealbreaker
5
Insurance Empanelments — Ownership
"Regarding the 25+ insurance networks — are all empanelment agreements held in the company name rather than personally? And are they transferable to a new owner on change of ownership without requiring fresh applications?"
🔴 Red if: Any major payer (DAMAN, AXA) is in personal name or has suspended status · 🟢 Green if: All in company name, transferableDealbreaker
6
Legal Proceedings
"Are there any current or pending legal proceedings — including medical malpractice claims, patient complaints upheld by the DHA, labour disputes, or regulatory penalties — that we should be aware of?"
🔴 Red if: Any unresolved malpractice >AED 500K or active DHA investigation · 🟢 Green if: No proceedings, or minor resolved disputes with documentationDealbreaker

Round 2 — Post-NDA · Full Financial & Operational DD · Allow 2 Weeks
Strategy: NDA is signed. Now you go deeper. These questions are more technical — frame them as "standard process for a transaction of this size." Answers here will either confirm or adjust the AED 85M valuation.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 2
Thank you for your continued cooperation — buyer interest remains strong and we are making good progress. Now that the NDA is in place, there are a few areas we'd like to go a little deeper on. Could you share a copy of the tenancy agreement, including the expiry date and renewal terms? Buyers will also want to know whether landlord consent is required on a change of ownership. On the financials, a brief breakdown of the owner's withdrawals across FY2023 and FY2024 — between salary, dividends, and personal expenses — would allow us to present the true earnings power of the business accurately. Please also share a summary of any transactions with related parties, if applicable. From a cash flow perspective, an approximate picture of insurance receivables aging and the average claims rejection rate would be helpful. On the assets side, could you help us reconcile the small difference between the asset register and the audited accounts? Finally, a summary of which doctors are on formal employment contracts versus visiting arrangements, along with confirmation that all hold current DHA clinical privilege certificates, would complete this phase. Please also confirm whether the Credence HMS license is transferable.
1
Lease Agreement
"Could you share a copy of the current tenancy contract — including landlord name, lease expiry, annual rent, renewal terms, and whether landlord consent is required for a change of ownership?"
🔴 Red if: Lease expires within 18 months with no renewal guarantee · 🟢 Green if: 3+ years remaining, no consent requiredDealbreaker
2
Owner Drawings Breakdown
"The owner's current account shows AED 7.58M withdrawn in FY2024 and AED 4.81M in FY2023. Could you provide a breakdown — official salary, profit dividends, or personal expenses run through the company?"
This changes normalised EBITDA significantly and is your valuation defence at AED 85M.Important
3
Related Party Transactions
"Could you provide a schedule of all transactions with related parties — any payments to companies or individuals connected to the owner or management team?"
Owner-connected suppliers may be inflating costs. If removed post-sale, margins improve.Important
4
Aged Debtor Schedule
"Could you share the current accounts receivable aging schedule? Specifically, what percentage of outstanding insurance claims are beyond 90 days?"
Old unpaid insurance claims are a hidden cash flow risk buyer will inherit on day one.Important
5
Insurance Claims Rejection Rate
"What is the average insurance claim rejection rate across your payer networks, and what is the average time from service delivery to payment receipt?"
Industry average in Dubai is 8-12%. Above that signals a billing problem.Important
6
Asset Register Reconciliation
"The asset register shows AED 26.1M while audited accounts show AED 33.5M. Could you explain the AED 7.4M difference?"
This gap must be resolved before any buyer signs.Important
7
Doctor Contracts
"Could you share the staff schedule showing which doctors are on employment contracts, which are part-time, and which are visiting doctors with no exclusivity?"
Visiting doctors can walk away the day after sale. Buyer needs to know how much surgical volume is secured.Important
8
DHA Clinical Privilege Certificates
"Could you provide copies of DHA clinical privilege certificates for each of the 36 doctors — confirming privileges match the procedures being performed?"
A doctor performing procedures outside DHA privileges is a serious regulatory violation.Legal
9
Hospital Management System
"Is the Credence software license transferable to a new owner without additional fees or reapplication?"
Patient records, billing history, and insurance submissions are all in this system.Commercial
10
Fixed Cost Floor
"What would monthly cash requirement be if surgical volume dropped by 40% for 90 days — what are the fixed costs that must be paid regardless of revenue?"
Buyer needs to know worst-case monthly burn during transition period.Commercial

Round 3 — Pre-Negotiation · Legal & Commercial Terms · Week 5–6
Strategy: DD is substantially complete. Now you shape the deal structure and protect the buyer. These questions directly feed into the SPA terms, earnout clauses, and price adjustments.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 3
We are entering the final preparation phase before formal negotiations begin, and things are looking very positive. There are a few important areas we need your input on to structure the deal correctly. Could you please share the Memorandum of Association and current shareholding structure? We also need confirmation that there are no charges or pledges on the company's assets beyond the bank facility already disclosed. For key supplier relationships valued above AED 200,000 annually, copies or a brief summary of those contracts would be helpful. On the regulatory side, could you confirm who holds the named DHA Responsible Pharmacist position and whether they intend to stay? We'd also appreciate your view on the preferred deal structure — share sale or asset sale — and whether you'd be open to a portion of the consideration being linked to surgical volumes post-completion. Lastly, please confirm which senior staff you would commit to retaining for 18 months post-completion.
1
Corporate Documents
"Could you share the full corporate documents — MOA, current shareholding structure, and any shareholder agreements?"
Legal counsel needs this to structure the transaction.Legal
2
Undisclosed Charges on Assets
"Beyond the AED 3.5M bank loan, are there any other charges, mortgages, or pledges registered against the company's assets or shares?"
Any undisclosed charge is a dealbreaker — buyer inherits it.Dealbreaker
3
Supplier Contracts
"Could you provide copies of all supplier contracts exceeding AED 200,000 annually and confirm which are transferable to a new owner?"
Personal supply arrangements vanish on sale.Commercial
4
Responsible Pharmacist
"Who is the named DHA Responsible Pharmacist and is this person willing to remain with the business post-acquisition?"
UAE law requires a named pharmacist. If they leave and no replacement is approved, the pharmacy closes.Legal
5
OT Utilisation Rate
"What is the current OT utilisation rate — hours used versus hours available per week for each of the 3 operating theatres?"
Low utilisation = growth upside. High utilisation = limited room without infrastructure investment.Commercial
6
Deal Structure Preference
"What is your preferred transaction structure — a share sale or an asset sale? Are you aware of the UAE corporate tax implications of each structure for both parties?"
Share sale transfers all liabilities. Asset sale is cleaner for buyer.Legal
7
Earnout & Warranty
"Would you be open to an earnout structure where a portion of the consideration is paid over 24 months tied to surgical volume maintaining 250+ procedures per month?"
Earnout protects the buyer if surgical volume drops post-sale.Commercial
8
Key Staff Retention
"Which senior staff — Operations Manager, Accounts Manager, Insurance Coordinator — are you prepared to contractually commit to retaining for 18 months post-completion?"
Continuity of key staff and insurer relationships is critical in the first 6 months.Commercial

Strategy: Everything is agreed in principle. These are the final bring-down confirmations before contracts are exchanged.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 4
We are at the final stage and everything is progressing well. This note is simply to confirm a few closing items before we proceed to contract exchange. Could you please confirm, through your legal counsel, that all DHA licenses, insurance empanelments, and key supplier agreements are transferable to the buyer without penalty? We will also need a confirmed picture of the cash and working capital position as of the proposed completion date. Regarding the bank facility, please arrange written confirmation from the bank that all registered charges will be released upon repayment from the sale proceeds. A brief written confirmation that there have been no material changes to the business — surgical volumes, doctor headcount, or insurance status — since the last accounts provided would also be required as standard. Finally, please share your legal counsel's full contact details and confirm they are authorised to exchange contracts within five business days of final agreed terms.

Buyer Q&A Preparation — Questions a Buyer Will Ask YOU
Purpose: These are the hard questions any serious buyer — PE firm, hospital group, family office — will fire at you. Your job is to have a prepared, confident answer ready.
B1
Can you verify the 280 surgeries/month with audited data?
This is the most important question. The entire IP revenue reconstruction depends on it.Dealbreaker
B2
What is the normalised EBITDA after adding back owner drawings?
Reported EBITDA AED 6.3M looks low for AED 85M ask. Normalised EBITDA ~AED 13.3M justifies it.Important
B3
Why is the asset register AED 7.4M less than the audited books?
Any serious buyer will spot this immediately. You need an explanation ready.Important
B4
Are insurance empanelments transferable on change of ownership?
If they're not, the business loses its primary revenue channel on day one.Dealbreaker
B5
Who is the key-man and what happens if he leaves?
99% owned by one person. Buyer's biggest concern after the numbers.Important
B6
Why was the valuation done internally with no named firm?
No firm letterhead, no independence declaration — buyer's advisors will flag this immediately.Important
B7
What is the FY2025 revenue trend?
FY2024 is now 17+ months old. Buyer wants to know if the business is growing or declining today.Important
B8
What is the DHA license transfer process and timeline?
Buyer needs to plan for the 60-90 day DHA process in their completion timeline.Legal
B9
What happened to the AED 7.58M owner drawings — was profit being hidden?
A sharp buyer or their FDD team will ask this directly.Important
B10
What is the insurance claims rejection rate?
If above industry average (8-12%), buyer will use it to reduce price.Commercial
B11
Why is AED 85M the right price when audited net profit is only AED 4.27M?
This is the central valuation challenge. Be ready to walk through the normalisation clearly.Dealbreaker
B12
What are the exit options in 5-7 years?
PE and family office buyers always think about exit before they enter.Commercial
