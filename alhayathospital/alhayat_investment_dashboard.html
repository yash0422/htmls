Round 1 — Pre-NDA · Send Before Signing Anything · 14-Day Deadline
Strategy: These 6 questions are conversational and non-threatening. Each one is a dealbreaker screen in disguise. If answers are not received within 14 days — treat as a red flag. If any answer is RED — stop and reassess before proceeding to NDA.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 1
I hope this message finds you well. As we progress with the sale process, there are a few points we'd like your input on — nothing formal at this stage, just helping us build a clearer picture. If you have any management figures for 2025, even a rough summary, that would help us understand the current trading position. Alongside this, a copy of the DHA hospital license would allow us to confirm the details and expiry date. We'd also appreciate a brief sense of your motivation for the sale — buyers always ask, and the more we understand, the better we can represent your interests. On that note, buyers we are speaking with are looking for a genuine partnership, not just a transaction. Having you available in an advisory capacity for 12 to 18 months post-completion would be a significant positive. On the operational side, a monthly breakdown of surgical cases for the last 12 months — from OT logs or insurance submissions — would allow us to present the volumes confidently. Finally, could you confirm whether the insurance empanelments are held in the company name and transferable on a change of ownership? Please share only what you are comfortable with at this stage. We look forward to your response.

Round 2 — Post-NDA · Full Financial & Operational DD · Allow 2 Weeks
Strategy: NDA is signed. Now you go deeper. These questions are more technical — frame them as "standard process for a transaction of this size." Answers here will either confirm or adjust the AED 85M valuation.
📋 Ready-to-Send Message — Round 2

Could you share a copy of the tenancy agreement, including the expiry date and renewal terms? Buyers will also want to know whether landlord consent is required on a change of ownership. On the financials, a brief breakdown of the owner's withdrawals across FY2023 and FY2024 — between salary, dividends, and personal expenses — would allow us to present the true earnings power of the business accurately. Please also share a summary of any transactions with related parties, if applicable. From a cash flow perspective, an approximate picture of insurance receivables aging and the average claims rejection rate would be helpful. Finally, a summary of which doctors are on formal employment contracts versus visiting arrangements, along with confirmation that all hold current DHA clinical privilege certificates, would complete this phase. Please also confirm whether the Credence HMS license is transferable. On the compliance side, could you confirm the company's VAT registration number and that all FTA returns are current with no outstanding assessments? Please also confirm UAE Corporate Tax registration and that the FY2024 return has been filed — and if possible, introduce us to your tax consultant so we can verify this directly. Finally, we would appreciate a full schedule of the employee gratuity liability — all staff by tenure, basic salary, and calculated entitlement under UAE Labour Law. Responsible Pharmacist

"What is the current OT utilisation rate by theatre — hours used versus hours available per week for each of the 3 operating theatres? This helps us model the upside potential and understand current capacity headroom."

"What is your preferred transaction structure — a share sale or an asset sale? And are you aware of the UAE corporate tax implications of each structure for both parties?"
Share sale transfers all liabilities. Asset sale is cleaner for buyer. Tax treatment differs — seller usually prefers share sale, buyer usually prefers asset sale.Legal
7
Earnout & Warranty
"Would you be open to an earnout structure where a portion of the consideration is paid over 24 months tied to surgical volume maintaining 250+ procedures per month? And are you willing to provide a 12-month warranty on the accuracy of financial information disclosed?"
Earnout protects the buyer if surgical volume drops post-sale. Warranty gives legal recourse if disclosed information turns out to be inaccurate.Commercial
8
Key Staff Retention
"Which senior staff members — Operations Manager, Accounts Manager, Insurance Coordinator — are you prepared to contractually commit to retaining for 18 months post-completion? And would you introduce the buyer to the top 5 insurance TPA relationships before completion?"
Continuity of key staff and insurer relationships is critical in the first 6 months. Loss of either is the biggest post-acquisition risk.Commercial

Gratuity Settlement — Method & Amount
"Based on the gratuity schedule provided, we would like to confirm the total liability and agree on the settlement method. Our preference is for the full gratuity liability to be settled by the seller from sale proceeds at completion, with a clean certificate from your HR/legal counsel confirming all obligations are discharged. If the seller prefers, we can alternatively structure a deduction from the purchase price at an agreed amount."
Once the quantum is known from Round 2, this becomes a negotiation item. A AED 3M gratuity liability reduces effective consideration from AED 85M to AED 82M — needs to be agreed and reflected in the SPA.Commercial

Competitive Differentiation
  • 1
    Aesthetic Surgery Leadership. 9 plastic surgeons and 280 surgeries/month positions this as the aesthetic surgery leader in Oud Metha. No competitor of similar scale competes in liposuction, rhinoplasty, or cosmetic procedures at this volume.
  • 2
    Mid-Market Price Point. American Hospital and Al Habib are premium-priced. This facility serves the insured middle market — the South Asian expatriate majority of Oud Metha's patient catchment — a segment premium competitors structurally cannot capture.
  • 3
    24/7 Pharmacy — Exclusive in Segment. No other mid-market facility in Oud Metha operates a round-the-clock pharmacy. This drives patient retention, repeat visits, and a revenue stream competitors don't capture.
  • 4
    Oud Metha Metro Station adjacency. The facility is directly adjacent to the Oud Metha Metro Station — the single most accessible healthcare location in central Dubai, simultaneously serving Bur Dubai, Karama, Deira, and DIFC catchments.
  • 5
    Replacement cost barrier. Any buyer entering Oud Metha faces AED 150M–200M to build a new hospital from scratch, 18–24 months for DHA licensing, and years to build 25,000 patient relationships. Acquiring this asset at AED 85M is the only viable entry strategy.
